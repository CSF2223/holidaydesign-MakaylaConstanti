Christmas

Christmas is a yearly celebration honoring the birth of Jesus Christ that is celebrated by billions of people all over the world on December 25 as a religious and cultural holiday. Christmas Day is a public holiday in many nations. It is a religious holiday for the majority of Christians and a cultural holiday for many non-Christians. It also plays a significant role in the holiday season that is centered around it. Christmas is incredibly popular everywhere and is observed in a variety of various ways around the world. For instance People in India attend special midnight masses and prepare the delicious pudding kheer for their friends and relatives. On Christmas Eve, the Norwegians indulge in a unique rice pudding dish called riskrem.

Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year begins on the second new moon following the winter solstice and is also known as Lunar New Year in many places outside of China. The precise day differs throughout the year, but the season is always winter. The holiday is weeks long in China! Chinese New Year celebrations come in a variety of shapes and sizes, from massive public fireworks displays and thrilling lion dances to private family dinners at home and giving money in red envelopes to youngsters for good luck. Every year, this lively, prosperous celebration with ancient origins provides happiness and warmth to winter!
